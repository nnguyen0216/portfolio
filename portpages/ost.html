oSTEM Designs


Goal: To attract potential members to the organization during tabling events, highlight what oSTEM is about through visuals instead of words, and promote a safe space.

Timeline: January 2018 - November 2018

The Process: I worked with the oSTEM @ UCSD board members during each step of the production process in order to receive direction on what the banners, brochures, and website branding should look like. At the initial meeting, to gauge what direction they were going towards in terms of designs, the presidents + VP + I sketched out layouts for the brochures and potential backgrounds for the panels.

To represent the mission statement of oSTEM, I suggested that we create a background that had elements of STEM colored in with a rainbow gradient. My goal was: when members come up to grab a brochure during a tabling event on library walk (where it is very packed with people), they will be able to understand what oSTEM’s mission is without a verbal explanation, leading to the feeling of privacy and a safer space.






Rainbow elements are included in every design. For banners, which are large and hung right in the front of the recruitment table, I incorporated watercolor brushes to deliver a “soft” effect, further adding to the feeling of a safe space.



Color palette: I chose to use the exact palette used in the oSTEM logo in order to make the designs come full circle. For backgrounds, however, I used a more pastel version of these colors so that it would not compete with other parts of the brochure.
